Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

Scroll to view timeline • Hover or tap cards to learn more

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the first commercial microprocessor—igniting the microprocessor revolution and propelling the future of computing.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that powers countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Marks Intel’s highest annual operational GHG emissions. Subsequent years bring investment in abatement, renewables, and efficient manufacturing to reverse the trend.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches RISE and 2030 goals, driving progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction across the value chain.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Commitment to achieve net-zero Scope 1 & 2 GHG emissions across global operations by 2040, building on years of environmental initiatives.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Achieves ~99% renewable electricity usage worldwide—dramatically lowering operational emissions and supporting clean energy markets.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, bringing suppliers, officials, and industry leaders together to collaborate on greener semiconductor manufacturing.
